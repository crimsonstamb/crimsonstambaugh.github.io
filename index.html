Publications
Mixed Density Diffuser: Efficient Planning with Non-Uniform Temporal Resolution
Crimson Stambaugh, Rajesh Rao
ESANN, 2026
arXiv

To mitigate both planning degradation from sparse planning and overfitting or excessive model parameter counts from continuous dense planning, we proposed Mixed Density Diffuser (MDD): a flexible diffusion planning framework for offline RL that treats temporal trajectory sparsity as a tunable hyperparameter. By optimizing these densities, MDD surpassed existing frameworks to achieve a new state-of-the-art on the D4RL benchmark.

Multi-Modal Natural Intelligence through Active Predictive Coding
Jeffrey Duan, Vishwas Sathish, Crimson Stambaugh, Rajesh Rao
AAAI “Neuro for AI & AI for Neuro: Towards Multi-Modal Natural Intelligence” Workshop, 2026
arXiv (coming soon)

Active predictive coding (APC) is a recently proposed theory of the neocortex that postulates that a canonical sensory-motor processing circuit is replicated across cortical areas. We validate APC's potential as a unifying framework for multi-modal intelligence by demonstrating that the same APC architecture can perform visual object recognition, plan hierarchically for navigation, parse language to infer and achieve goals through actions, and scale to realistic environments.
